## Introduction to Glimmer
We just spotted an exciting update from Google Design that's worth sharing with the community - the introduction of Glimmer, a new design language for UX on glasses. Here's what caught our attention about this: Glimmer prioritizes voice, gesture, and eye-tracking, focusing on glanceable, transient elements that appear only when needed.

## Key Features of Glimmer
The community is buzzing about the potential of Glimmer to transform the way we interact with smart glasses. Some of the key features of Glimmer include:
* Prioritization of voice, gesture, and eye-tracking
* Glanceable, transient elements that appear only when needed
* Thoughtful design for a new dimension

## Implications and Potential Use Cases
What's really exciting about Glimmer is its potential to revolutionize the field of UX design and beyond. Here's why this matters for developers: Glimmer's focus on voice, gesture, and eye-tracking could enable the creation of more intuitive and seamless user experiences. The implications are vast, with potential use cases in fields such as:
* Healthcare: smart glasses for medical professionals
* Education: interactive learning experiences
* Gaming: immersive gaming experiences
