Google AI today rolled out **Nano Banana 2 Lite**, a Gemini image model that returns text‑to‑image results in under 4 seconds, and **Gemini Omni Flash**, a public‑preview multimodal model for cheap video generation. The announcement was detailed in a [Google AI tweet](https://x.com/i/status/2071989058952188054) and a demo app is available at the [AI Studio link](http://goo.gle/443xPqw).

## Faster image generation
Nano Banana 2 Lite is positioned as the fastest Gemini image model yet. It hits sub‑4‑second latency via the Gemini API and Google AI Studio, and is being integrated into products like Notebook LM and Google Photos. For startups that need quick visual prototypes, the speed can shave minutes off an iteration loop.

## Video and conversational editing in one model
Gemini Omni Flash adds cost‑efficient video generation and conversational editing capabilities. It is offered in public preview through the Gemini API, AI Studio, and the Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform, enabling developers to embed video creation directly into apps.

## Access and pricing considerations
Both models are billed through the Gemini API. Google claims they run at “a fraction of the cost,” but exact pricing isn’t public yet. Early adopters should monitor usage to avoid surprise bills, especially when generating high‑resolution video frames.

## Limitations and cautions
The models are still in early rollout. Omni Flash is only in public preview, so stability and feature completeness may vary. Image outputs can exhibit occasional artifacts, and video generation may produce jitter on complex scenes, leading to extra post‑processing work.

**What to watch:** Keep an eye on the official pricing page and test the demo app for your specific use case. If you need rapid visual mock‑ups or cheap video clips for a product demo, try Nano Banana 2 Lite and Omni Flash now, but allocate a small budget for unexpected API costs.
