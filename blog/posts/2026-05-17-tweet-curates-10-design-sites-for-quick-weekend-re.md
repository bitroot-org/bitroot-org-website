On May 17, 2026, a tweet from @tranmautritam shared a list of ten design‑focused websites, each described in a single line, as a ready‑to‑use weekend stash. The post can be seen in [the tweet](https://x.com/i/status/2055922458876608947).

## The sites at a glance
- **wirtetext.app** – a Unicode wireframe tool that exports plain‑text sketches.
- **smallbits.design** – over 290 pixel‑perfect icons on a tight grid.
- **designspells.com** – a catalog of micro‑interactions harvested from real apps.
- **maxibesto.one/typefaces** – curated fonts pulled from production websites.
- **typewolf.com** – font identification on top sites plus weekly type picks.
- **fontastic.space** – typography used in movies and cinema.
- **anothergraphic.org** – archive of graphic design and typographic treatments.
- **awwards.com/awwards/collections/free-fonts** – free fonts for web projects.
- **godly.website** – showcase of award‑quality web design.
- **lawsofux.com** – UX principles presented like a book.

## Why it matters to shipping engineers
These resources are lightweight, web‑based, and mostly free, making them easy to drop into a sprint without procurement overhead. A wireframe tool like *wirtetext.app* can replace a quick sketch in Figma when you need to prototype an API endpoint UI in minutes. Icon sets and font libraries reduce the time spent searching for assets that match a brand style.

## Cost and licensing snapshot
All listed sites offer a free tier; some, such as *typewolf.com* or *maxibesto.one*, have paid plans for commercial font licenses. Because the tools are SaaS or static catalogs, there are no upfront license fees, but be aware of per‑project attribution requirements that can add friction for closed‑source products.

## Cautionary notes
The list is curated by a single individual, so coverage is anecdotal. Asset quality varies—some icons lack accessibility metadata, and font licenses may restrict embedding. Treat the sites as a starting point, not a definitive source of production‑ready assets.

**What to watch:** Bookmark the tweet, trial the free wireframe and icon sites during your next design spike, and verify any font license before committing it to a shipped product.
