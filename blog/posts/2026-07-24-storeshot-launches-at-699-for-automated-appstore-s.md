StoreShot is now available for a one‑time price of $6.99, positioning itself as a cheap alternative to the $25 per‑month AppScreens service. The tool was built by KM after finding the existing UX unsatisfactory, and it integrates with an AI Agent via MCP to seed design ideas and export ready‑to‑publish images.

## How StoreShot works
StoreShot lets developers upload a mockup or design file, then uses an AI Agent to generate a set of device‑framed screenshots. The agent can be prompted to follow brand guidelines, add overlays, or tweak layouts before the final export. Once the images are ready, they can be pushed directly to the Apple App Store Connect or Google Play Console if the user has linked the appropriate APIs.

## Cost comparison and value
At $6.99 a month (or a one‑time fee, according to the announcement), StoreShot is roughly 72 % cheaper than the $25 monthly fee of AppScreens. The lower price makes it attractive for indie developers and small startups that need frequent screenshot updates for ASO experiments. The tool’s pricing model also avoids recurring bills, which can simplify budgeting.

## Limitations to consider
While the AI‑assisted workflow speeds up creation, it can produce generic visual elements that may not pass every store’s review guidelines without manual tweaking. The integration with MCP is optional; teams without an AI Agent will need to perform the design steps manually. Additionally, because StoreShot is a newer service, it lacks the polish and support ecosystem of more established competitors.

## When to try it
Developers who are already paying for screenshot generation services and are comfortable experimenting with AI‑driven design should test StoreShot on a low‑stakes app update. A quick trial can reveal whether the UX improvements and cost savings outweigh the need for occasional manual adjustments.

**What to watch** – Keep an eye on updates to the MCP integration and any new export formats that might broaden StoreShot’s compatibility with emerging app‑store requirements.
