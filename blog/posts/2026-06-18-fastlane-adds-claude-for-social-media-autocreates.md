Fastlane announced **Claude for Social Media**, a feature that lets you paste a website URL and receive thousands of short promotional videos in one click, according to the [tweet](https://x.com/i/status/2067501867198996528).

## What the tool does
Claude uses a large language model to script, edit, and render video snippets that highlight product features, screenshots, or copy from the supplied site. The output is packaged as ready‑to‑post clips for platforms like TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts. The promise is "viral‑ready" content without manual editing.

## Pricing and resource usage
Fastlane has not published a detailed price sheet yet, but the marketing copy suggests a per‑video or per‑batch billing model. Early adopters may see a free trial with a limited number of videos before hitting a pay‑as‑you‑go tier. Expect higher compute costs if you generate large batches, which can add up quickly for startups on tight budgets.

## Caveats and noise
Automated scripts often produce generic or repetitive footage, leading to lower engagement than handcrafted clips. Because the model pulls text verbatim from the site, any brand‑specific nuances or compliance language may be missed, creating false‑positive claims. Additionally, the "thousands" claim is a best‑case scenario; actual output quality may vary widely across product types.

## When to experiment
If your startup already spends hours manually cutting short videos, try Claude on a single campaign to benchmark conversion rates against your current workflow. Keep an eye on the cost per generated minute and the engagement metrics; abandon the tool if the ROI falls below your acquisition cost threshold.

**What to watch:** Fastlane plans to release analytics dashboards next quarter, which could help quantify the true impact of AI‑generated video on click‑through and share rates.
